    Generative AI Architecture Trends¶

    The following prompt was given to OpenAI's Deep Research tool.

    Prompt

    Please use the "Deep Research" feature to find out the key trends on how enterprise architects are using generative AI as a force multiplier to have larger impact within their organizations. Generate a list of the top 10 trends and then provide detailed references in the following format:

    1. Site Title - publication_date - publication_name - description of how this site is related to the use of generative AI in enterprise architecture

    Response¶

    ChatGPT Said — GPT-5 Thinking ChatGPT thought for 45 seconds.

    Here’s a concise, research-driven view of how enterprise architects (EAs) are using generative AI as a force multiplier.

    Top 10 trends EAs are leaning into¶

    1. Agentic AI moves from pilots to platform features. EAs are planning for multi-agent patterns, persistent memory, and agent-to-agent standards as vendors roadmap “agentic” capabilities into mainstream software. (Gartner)
    2. “Continuous EA”: living, real-time architecture models. AI augments EA with telemetry-fed, machine-readable models and copilots that advise, validate, and even act—shifting EA from episodic reviews to continuous governance. (Forrester)
    3. AI-augmented EA tooling becomes standard. Leading EAMS vendors embed GenAI for inventory building, modeling, documentation, reasoning (e.g., MCP gateways), and governance—multiplying EA throughput. (leanix.net)
    4. Enterprise-wide AI governance anchored in EA. EAs stand up policies, controls, catalogs, and lineage to de-risk AI at scale; dedicated EA-led AI management solutions are emerging. (ardoq.com)
    5. Data fabric & knowledge layers as AI context backbones. To curb hallucinations and enable agent orchestration, EAs prioritize unified, governed data fabrics/knowledge graphs over fragmented data silos. (TechRadar)
    6. Open, modular AI reference architectures. EAs design reusable services with APIs plus “policy-as-code” and “infra-as-code” to swap models/providers and scale reuse across domains. (McKinsey & Company)
    7. Core modernization with an “intelligent core.” GenAI is woven into ERP/core systems and modernization programs, with EA steering where AI augments processes vs. replaces them. (Deloitte)
    8. Value-linked roadmaps & portfolio rationalization. EA uses GenAI to accelerate capability mapping, scenario design, and tech-debt reduction—tying AI initiatives to measurable business outcomes. (Gartner)
    9. Pragmatism on impact metrics. After early hype, EAs emphasize integration with workflows and measurable P\&L impact; most value comes when AI is applied to specific, automatable back-office work. (Tom's Hardware)
    10. Skills & methods refresh within EA frameworks. TOGAF-aligned guidance and skills updates reflect AI-era roles (governance, building-block selection, sustainability)—raising EA’s strategic profile. (blog.opengroup.org)
